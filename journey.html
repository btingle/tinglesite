Wavy Wobbly Thing

This was a deeper dive into creating custom gcode- the free software I use approximates your mesh by tracing contours around it Z-level by Z-level, or X-level by X-level, etc, but never traces contours that combine motions from all axes, even though the machine has the capability. If your geometry follows a natural contour, it makes more sense for the gcode to follow that natural contour than to try and approximate it level by level. It is much more efficient, too- this quality of surface finish would have taken hours instead of minutes to achieve if the default gcode was used.

CNC Workshop- Yin Yang Trays

I hosted a workshop for the CNC machine with four students, where each student would be guided through the steps of creating a project idea I had prepared in advance. The project was to create a set of matching yin-yang trays for holding miscellaneous small items. I showed by example how to perform each operation and explained its importance, and then let each student perform it themselves, correcting mistakes if need be and explaining what had gone wrong. Despite significant overtime due to some unexpectedly faulty tools, each student walked home satisfied with a completed project. The goal was to spark interest amongst the interested by providing a short but sweet example on what the machine is capable of doing.
